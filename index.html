@newCoders

Getting the first job is the most tenuous and important step in a programmer's career. There's a perceived risk in hiring a candidate with no professional coding experience, but one way to decrease that risk is by collaborating with professional developers who will mentor you, act as a reference, and give you the experience of working on professional calibre projects.

@proCoders

We all have side projects, but it's near impossible to complete anything more complex than a simple plugin. There just isn't enough time in the week. So why not have a new coder looking to break into the industry work on your side project. Your big idea gets built, and you get to mentor up-and-coming coders at the most important time of their careers.
